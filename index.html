Publications

Estimation of Soil Loss using RUSLE, GIS, and Remote Sensing

Journal of Agricultural Engineering (India), 2023

This study integrates the Revised Universal Soil Loss Equation (RUSLE) with GIS and multi-source remote sensing data to quantify spatial and temporal patterns of soil erosion in Sangli district, Maharashtra. Landsat imagery, DEM-derived terrain parameters, rainfall erosivity, soil erodibility, crop management, and conservation practice factors were systematically processed and modeled to generate soil loss estimates. The results provide actionable insights for sustainable land resource management, erosion control planning, and policy-level decision-making in agriculturally vulnerable regions.

Waterbody Mapping using Modified 3-Component Decomposition of TerraSAR-X

IGARSS 2026 (Accepted)

This work proposes a modified three-component model-based decomposition approach for improved waterbody extraction using high-resolution TerraSAR-X SAR data. The methodology enhances discrimination between water surfaces and surrounding land features under complex scattering and surface conditions. The proposed approach demonstrates improved accuracy compared to conventional SAR-based techniques, highlighting its potential for reliable water resource monitoring, hydrological analysis, and environmental applications in data-scarce or cloud-prone regions.
